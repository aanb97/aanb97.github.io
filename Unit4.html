Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

This unit explained the concepts of case studies, focus groups and the methods used for observation, while highlighting how these methods can be used in my research. These tools are essential for acquiring profound understanding of your research topic and how to collect the needed information surrounding that topic.

Focus groups: Focus groups are usually in-person interviews with a limited number of participants who have been pre-selected based on their demographics. Their responses to certain queries presented by researchers or evaluators are examined.

Case studies:

An extensive, in-depth analysis of a specific instance in a real-world setting is called a case study. It is employed to draw conclusions and generalizations and might concentrate on a person, an organization, group or an event. A typical respondents may be included in the survey, which is another reason why case studies are not a trustworthy method of performing descriptive research.

Observation:

The objective approach of collecting data is the quantitative observation, whereas the subjective method is a qualitative observation.
