Unit 6: Quantitative Methods - Descriptive and Inferential Statistics

This unit disucussed the quantitative method and defined the different levels in such data. We also learned how produce measures of location and spread for the data.

I developed a thorough understanding of a variety of data collection techniques during my studies. These techniques are essential for collecting precise and trustworthy data for study and analysis.

A list of a dataset's salient features is given by descriptive statistics. This comprises dispersion (e.g., range, variance, standard deviation) and location (e.g., mean, median, mode). It is necessary to comprehend these ideas in order to successfully analyze and communicate facts.

The kinds of data that can be used and the suitable statistical analysis that can be performed depend on the measurement levels. Data with varying degrees of precision are represented by nominal, ordinal, interval, and ratio data types.

I have gained proficiency in the analysis and interpretation of data from a variety of sources by using descriptive statistics and taking measurement levels into account. This information is essential for carrying out fruitful research and coming to wise decisions.
