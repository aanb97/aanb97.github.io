Unit 6: Quantitative Methods - Descriptive and Inferential Statistics

This unit disucussed the quantitative method and defined the different levels in such data. We also learned how produce measures of location and spread for the data.

Descriptive statistics:

Descriptive statistics are used for characterizing and summarizing aspects of a set of data. Additionally, it organizes and analyzes data without drawing broad conclusions. It does this by using metrics like central tendency and variability to comprehend the features of the dataset.

Inferential statistics:

Inferential statistics are used in utilizing a sample of data from a population to make assumptions or predictions about that group. Furthermore, they are used for making inferences or forecasts about a wider population based on sampling data. Also they are used for Examining a hypothesis's odds, presumptions, and results

Difference between Inferential and descriptive statistics:

A data set's features are summed up by descriptive statistics. Using inferential statistics, you may determine whether your data can be applied to a larger population or test a hypothesis.
